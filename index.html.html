About Me

Who I Am

Hi, I'm Harry Sargent - a Gold Coast-based Griffith University student currently completing my Bachelor of Business, majoring in Economics. I grew up on the Gold Coast and have always been drawn to understanding how systems work: why economies behave the way they do, how policy decisions ripple through communities, and what it takes to build institutions that genuinely serve people.

Outside of my studies, I've built a career in client-facing sales roles that have taught me things no textbook can: how to listen carefully, how to earn trust quickly, and how to communicate complex ideas in ways that actually land. I've worked in everything from clinical pathology to travel consulting, and each role has sharpened a different edge of who I am professionally.

I'm someone who takes ideas seriously. I read broadly across economics, policy, history and philosophy, and I find myself most energised when I'm working on problems that matter - questions about how we allocate resources, design institutions, and make collective decisions that affect real people's lives.

This ePortfolio is a snapshot of where I am right now: a student with genuine curiosity, a growing set of skills, and a clear sense of where I want to go. I hope it gives you a sense not just of what I've done, but of who I am and what I care about.

2

Open Economy Macroeconomics - IMF Policy Briefing Report (2025)

Analytical Thinking

A formal policy briefing report completed for 3310AFE, applying open-economy macroeconomic frameworks to a real-world policy scenario. Demonstrates capacity for rigorous economic analysis and structured academic writing.

Reflection

Completing the IMF-style policy briefing for Open Economy Macroeconomics was the most formative academic experience of my studies to date. The task required me to move beyond theoretical understanding and apply macroeconomic frameworks - including balance of payments dynamics, exchange rate mechanics, and fiscal sustainability - to a concrete policy problem. Constructing a coherent, evidence-based argument under strict structural and word-count constraints taught me that rigour and clarity are not in conflict; they reinforce each other. More importantly, it deepened my conviction that economics is most valuable when placed in service of real decisions and real communities. This artefact reflects who I want to become professionally: someone who can hold complexity, communicate it honestly, and contribute meaningfully to the policy debates that shape people's lives.

Career Aspiration

Where I Am Headed

I am a Bachelor of Business (Economics) student with a genuine commitment to using economic analysis as a tool for better public outcomes. My studies have equipped me with a rigorous understanding of macroeconomic frameworks, fiscal policy, and open-economy dynamics - knowledge I am eager to apply in roles where evidence shapes real decisions that affect real communities.

My aspiration is to begin my career within the public sector or a policy research environment, contributing to the kind of careful, honest analysis that underpins sound government decision-making. I am particularly drawn to areas of fiscal sustainability, labour market reform, and the structural transitions facing the Australian economy - challenges that demand both technical competence and a genuine sense of civic responsibility.

I believe good policy is built on intellectual humility, an openness to complexity, and a willingness to interrogate comfortable assumptions. Over time, I aim to grow into a role where I can contribute meaningfully to policy design and public debate - not merely as a technician, but as someone who understands that economics, at its best, is a discipline in service of human wellbeing.
